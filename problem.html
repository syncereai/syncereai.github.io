The hotel industry is facing a massive labor problem. They can’t find enough staff to keep up, and they’re paying more than ever to try to fix it.

On average, hotels spend $50,000 per room annually on operations, and 50% of that goes to labor costs. For a typical 200-room hotel, that’s $5M per year spent on labor alone. Yet 76% of hotels are still understaffed, with housekeeping roles being the hardest to fill.

What makes matters worse is that hotels have increased wages by 20% in recent years to entice people to work in housekeeping, but it hasn’t been enough. There are still 100k+ vacant housekeeping jobs in the U.S. alone currently, because people don’t want physically demanding, low-paying jobs with little to no career growth.

We’re building robots to solve this problem for hotels.

Our robots will perform all core housekeeping tasks (making beds, cleaning bathrooms, tidying rooms, etc.) at a cost of $1,000 per month, which is 3–4x cheaper than a human housekeeper.

These robots are ready to work around the clock to help hotels operate more efficiently, improve guest experiences, and increase profits.
